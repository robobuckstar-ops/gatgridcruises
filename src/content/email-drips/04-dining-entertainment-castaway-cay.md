A Disney cruise has a *lot* going on. After hundreds of bookings, three
things separate guests who say "that was incredible" from guests who say
"that was nice." All three are free, once you know to look for them.

### 1. Rotational dining is the magic, not the gimmick

Most cruise lines hand you a buffet and an upcharge steakhouse. Disney moves
you (and your servers) through three themed restaurants over the week. Your
server learns your kids' names by night two, knows that your spouse hates
mushrooms by night three, and is bringing dessert before you ask by night
four.

The themed shows inside the restaurants are show-quality. On the Wish, your
*Frozen* dinner at **Arendelle** has live performers and a full storyline.
On the Treasure, **Plaza de Coco** turns dinner into a Día de los Muertos
musical with live mariachi. **Animator's Palate** ends with the walls
literally coming alive on every ship.

You don't book these. They're included. Just show up to your assigned
dining time.

### 2. Adult-exclusive dining is the best $45 you'll spend

**Palo** is on every Disney ship. Northern Italian, adult-only, $45 per
person, dress code (jacket suggested). Three-course menu of legitimately
great food in a quiet room with a view.

Drop the kids at the Oceaneer Club at 5:30, dinner at 6, pick up at 9.
You've just had a date night in the middle of a family vacation.

**Adult brunch at Palo** (also $45) is the move on sea days. Same room,
brighter, prosecco-friendly. Books out within hours of your window opening,
so set a midnight alarm.

If you're celebrating something big — anniversary, milestone birthday —
**Remy** (Dream/Fantasy) or **Enchanté** (Wish/Treasure/Destiny) at ~$135
per person are special-occasion territory. Skip them otherwise.

### 3. Castaway Cay is the day your trip becomes a memory

Disney's private island in the Bahamas. Almost every Caribbean and Bahamas
itinerary stops here. Plan it like a strategy, not a beach day:

- **Get off the ship in the first hour.** Loungers are first come, first
  served. Sleeping in costs you a good spot.
- **Family Beach, Pelican Plunge, or Serenity Bay** — pick one and commit.
  The trams are free but the day disappears fast.
- **Lunch at Cookie's BBQ** is free and surprisingly good. Get there before
  the noon rush.
- **Cabanas** are coveted (~$700+) and booked the moment your window opens.
  Either commit to it weeks in advance or let the idea go.
- **Bring water shoes.** The coral and rocky entries in some swim zones are
  rough on bare feet.

The newer Disney destination — **Lighthouse Point** at the south end of
Eleuthera — leans more "tropical retreat" than "theme park beach." Expect
it on Wish-class itineraries. Different vibe; same private-island guarantee
that the ship waits for you.

**Don't-miss entertainment list** (a few minutes of planning saves a lot of
"wish we'd known"):

- **Walt Disney Theatre evening show** — Broadway-caliber. Different on
  every ship.
- **Pirate Night deck party + fireworks at sea**. Bring red and black or
  dress up — kids will, and yours will feel left out if they don't.
- **Sail-Away Party** on the pool deck the afternoon you board. Champagne
  for adults.
- **Character meets** — book the popular ones (Anna & Elsa Royal Reception,
  Princess Gathering) in the Disney Cruise Line Navigator app pre-cruise.
