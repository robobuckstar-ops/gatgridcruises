Hi {{contact.FIRSTNAME | default: "there"}},

A question we get every week:

> *"If I can book a Disney cruise directly on the Disney website, why would
> I use a travel advisor?"*

Honest answer: most people **shouldn't**, if they're booking a basic 3-night
sample sailing and they enjoy spreadsheet work.

But for most families, using a Disney-focused advisor is genuinely free
upside. Three reasons why:

### 1. Same fare. Plus onboard credit.

Disney sets the cruise fare. It's identical whether you book direct, on the
Disney site, or through us. Disney pays the advisor's commission — not you.

What we *can* do is layer agency-level group amenities on top: usually
**$50–$300 in onboard credit**, sometimes a free specialty dinner, sometimes
both. That credit shows up on your folio the day you board. Buy a Palo
dinner, a few drinks, an excursion — already paid for.

### 2. Price-drop monitoring you don't have to do.

Disney's policy lets you reprice your sailing any time before paid-in-full.
The catch: you have to notice. Disney won't email you.

A good advisor watches your specific sailing daily and re-fares automatically
when the price drops. We've saved clients up to four figures on a single
cruise just by catching a Tuesday-morning price dip.

### 3. Booking windows we already know cold.

Adult dining, spa appointments, character meets, port adventures — they all
open in waves based on your Castaway Club tier (60, 90, 105, or 120 days out).
Miss the window for Palo Brunch and it'll be gone in an hour.

We're at the keyboard at midnight Eastern when your window opens. You're
asleep. That's the trade.
