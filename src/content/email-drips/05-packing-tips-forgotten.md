Quick one today. You already have the printable packing checklist in Chapter
4 of [the guide](https://gatgridcruises.com/disney-cruise-planning-guide.html).
This email is the stuff that doesn't show up on most lists — the items
returning cruisers swear by.

### The "wish I'd brought" hall of fame

- **Magnetic hooks (4–6 of them).** The stateroom walls are metal. Hang
  hats, swimsuits, lanyards, the daily Navigator schedule. $8 on Amazon.
  Genuinely transformative.
- **A non-surge power strip with USB.** Disney prohibits surge protectors
  (fire risk), but non-surge multi-outlet strips are allowed. Cabins have
  limited outlets — this saves the morning charging fight.
- **A lanyard for your Key to the World card.** Walking around with a
  credit-card-sized room key in a swimsuit pocket gets old fast.
- **A highlighter.** You'll get a paper Navigator schedule every night.
  Mark what your family is doing the next day in 30 seconds.
- **A small first aid / medicine kit.** Bonine, pain relievers, Band-Aids,
  Pepto. The onboard medical center is excellent — and very expensive.
- **Ziploc bags.** Wet swimsuits, beach trip from Castaway Cay, partially
  eaten Mickey pretzels. You won't miss them till you don't have them.
- **A reusable water bottle.** Free filtered water everywhere. Cuts down
  on running back to the cabin between Lido decks.
- **A light sweater or wrap.** Dining rooms run *cold*. The theatre runs
  colder.

### What you can skip

- **Don't bring an iron or steamer.** Both prohibited. Disney provides
  ironing rooms on every deck. Or pack a small bottle of **wrinkle-release
  spray** (Downy makes one).
- **Don't bring a beach towel.** Disney provides them at the pool decks and
  on port days. Saves a giant chunk of suitcase space.
- **Don't bring a hair dryer.** Every stateroom has one.
- **Don't bring formal-wear-formal-wear.** Even "dress-up night" on a
  Disney cruise is more "smart casual" than "tuxedo." Khakis and a button-
  down are completely fine for almost any dining room.

### The thing everyone wishes they'd done differently

**Pack a carry-on like you're flying internationally** — even if you're
driving to the port. Checked luggage often doesn't arrive at your stateroom
until 5 PM or later. Put swimsuits, sunscreen, medications, a change of
dinner clothes, and phone chargers in your carry-on. Spend embarkation day
in the pool instead of waiting for bags.

### Two pieces of paperwork

- **Passports.** Even for closed-loop sailings (return to same US port),
  passports are strongly recommended. If anything goes sideways — missed
  port, medical emergency, family crisis — you need them to fly home.
- **A printed copy of your reservation.** Phone batteries die at the worst
  moments. The terminal staff move much faster with paper in hand.
